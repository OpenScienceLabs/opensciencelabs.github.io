The Quantum Revolution: Resting and Running Together

In the 20th century, quantum physics brought a massive revolution. Until then, the scientific world operated on the rigid belief that atoms were indivisible and Newton’s laws were the absolute gospel. Quantum mechanics shattered that rigidity. Pioneering scientists like Niels Bohr, Max Planck, and Albert Einstein laid a monumental foundation to predict the behavior of microscopic particles invisible to the naked eye.

In regular electronics, there are only two options: either you run or you rest (1 or 0). But in the quantum world, you can do both simultaneously! It sounds a bit confusing, right? Look at it this way: you are sitting comfortably right now reading this article. But at this very microsecond, the Earth beneath you is hurtling around the Sun at an unbelievable speed. You are perfectly at rest, yet you are moving incredibly fast. In physics, this beautiful paradox is called Quantum Superposition, and it forms the bedrock of quantum computing.
